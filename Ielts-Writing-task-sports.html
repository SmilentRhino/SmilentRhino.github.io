The forth practice is about sports.

Some countries achieve international success by building specialized sports facilities for top athletes to train instead of providing sports facilities that everyone can use. Do you think it is a positive or negative development

My essay1:

As more countries attached their glory and influence with sports success, they tend to invest more on sports facilities, but instead of providing basic equipments and land for the public, the govenments choose to build professional train centers to support their top athletes. In my opinion, such specialized sports facilities release a positive signal and will improve people's awearness of the importance of sports.

To start with, top athlete benefits from these fancy and high-tech stadiums are more likely to win a better prize in international sports events, which will increase the proud among young generations and motivate them to exercise more. What's more, by doing this the whole society will recognize being an athletes as a respectfull job, a career worthy pursuing. The missing public facilities will be constructed by private companies because of the high profits in this industry.

Admittedly, this development has potentical risk of loss. If the elites fail to bring medals back home, we often would hear the complaints from the public, who might think it such a waste of their tax. However, in the long run this result is not likely to happen with more parents encouraging their children to engage in sports. The cost of maintainance of several top statiums are much less comparing with a nunmber natianwide basic facilities.

In conclusion, top sports training centers are neccessary for the development of top athletes, the long term benefits brought by these role models outweight the detriments.
