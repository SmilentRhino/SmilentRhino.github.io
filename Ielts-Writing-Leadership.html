The sixth writing practice, leadership

Question The leaders or directors of organizations are often older people, but some people say that young people can also be a leader. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

My Essay 1

Even though no evidene can prove there is a direct connection between age and leadership, people still prefer to be guided by a senior other than a young person. However we can see more and more younger leaders in emerging industries such as IT or electric cars. I fully support the idea that yong people can take the role as a good leader.

To start with, people favor older leader because they value the importance of experience. No matter how talent a young person can learn from books, he or she still need numerous real life practices to master the knowledge. Often, we call these practices experience, which can only be accumulated through years. It is difficult to build trust among the organization if one lack the experience, especially for the senior employees.

However, some people opt for younger leaders for a more aggressive leadship style. For companies that are experiencing fast development or expanding rapidly, conservetive strategy is what they least want. Young leaders who have not been through too much failure are inclined too be more adventurous to gain a first-mover advantage for the organizion in the market or competition.

In my opinion, we should not judge if a person if suitable to be a leader by age, just as we should not judge people by their appearance. But we have to admit people in different stage of their life have sometimes contrary leadership styles. Choose the right style and move towards success is the only thing that matters.
