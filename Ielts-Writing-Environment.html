My eighth ietls writing practice.

Nowadays both scientists and tourists can go to remote natural environments such as the South Pole. Do you think the advantages of this development outweigh the disadvantages?

Ideas

  • Human activity damaged the environment, that there was a civic duty to maintain the environment for future generations.
  • These scientific research will help us better understand our world, thus better protect them.
  • This unexplored placed are dangerous.
  • Tourists can experience an extraordinary side of nature

Positive impacts of tourism

  • Jobs for local people
  • Income for the local economy
  • Helps preserve rural services like buses, village shops and post offices
  • Increased demand for local food and crafts
  • Tourists mainly come to see the scenery and wildlife, so there is pressure to conserve habitats and wildlife

Negative impacts of tourism

  • Damage to the landscape: litter, erosion, fires, disturbance to livestock, vandalism
  • Traffic congestion and pollution
  • Local goods can become expensive because tourists will pay more
  • Shops stock products for tourists and not everyday goods needed by locals
  • Demand for holiday homes makes housing too expensive for local people
  • Demand for development of more shops and hotels
  • Jobs are mainly seasonal, low paid with long hours

My Essay 1

Some nature environments used to be uncharted and inaccessible such as the North Pole or antarctic are now frequectly visited by tourists for entertainment as well as scientists for study. Exposed to a rapid growing human presentence, some people worry that the unique biodiversity of these places are under great threat. In my opinion, as long as reasonable supervision are provided, this is still an exiting development we should welcome.

Admittedly, excessive human activities might bring risks to the environment. First, introduced species might have a negative impact on the ecosystem of these regions, or even totally destory it. Delibrately or not, new species introduced to these unique ecosystem could cause worse concequence than we expected. What's more, it is difficult to deal with the waste produced in these areas, while degration process of pollutions and waste are much more slower due to the low temperature. Therefore, stricter control should be placed on what is permitted to brought there and better pollution management is also needed.

However, leaving such environments alone will not really save them. Researches conducted in these area provide us a chance to better understand how the ecosystem works, which futher help us better protect it. For example, we have seen a decrease of antarctic sea ice in last decade caused by global warming. Through the study of these places, people could gain a better understanding of what is going on and adopt policies to tackle the problem.

In conclusion, exploration of remote nature places offer us some unique experiences and knowledge, and help use better protect these environments, provided we take mesures to control exceeded pollution.
